idealabs: review and updates

Posted by cedric on 24th of Januari 2013

idealabs: review and updates

To start off, we wish you a very happy 2013, may all your entrepreneurial wishes come true. 2013 is set to be a very big year for idealabs!

The ones that are following our social media channels already, are being updated on every step we, and our startups take. Thanks for being such great followers! Still, we thought it was time to give a written update on idealabs and our startups. Let’s take you through the last couple of months. An overview of our plans, the accelerator, and the startups that participated.

idealabs

We are very proud with what we achieved so far. We are running a very ambitious project, that requires a lot of work and patience. The return we’re getting from all that work is tremendous, that’s the reason why we are going to make 2013 a year never to forget. For entrepreneurs, by entrepreneurs! The idealabs mission: “Contributing to the Belgian Entrepreneurial Ecosystem” (operating from Antwerp) is still on. The key factor in that contribution is establishing a solid entrepreneurial community in Antwerp. First step in our proces was running our first accelerator program. We guided 3 startups during 3 months. We're running a new program from September 2013. We're reopening the application proces in April.
Second step is giving that community a physical place to unite. Our building is finally ready for that community. We have a coffeebar, a big event room, an accelerator and a crazy rooftop terrace (we even got a spare floor). That’s a giant step forward! Identifying and creating that community is key to make it work. We want to educate, inspire and engage people. Creating new entrepreneurs! A little explanation on these 3 drivers:


Educate: We strongly believe in education. That’s why we are rolling out a Startup Campus in our building. A mix between physical and digital education. The physical part of the campus is being put together as we speak. A series of classes/workshops that help your startup to move forward. The digital part, we are likely to become a viewing partner of General Assembly. Livestreaming their classes at our place.


Inspire: We need people to get inspired by the life as an entrepreneur. Past summer we had our Startup Sacrifices night, where we invited Edial Dekker and Danielle Schiffer from Berlin. Those were inspiring stories! We have more of those planned. We are even organising a big conference!


Engage: Time to stop talking and start building. Hackathons, Ideathons, Startup Weekends, Open Pitching Events, Matchmaking Events... Looks like a powerful cocktail!



Important note: we are not excluding anyone. We’re opening our HUB to everyone. Since innovation and entrepreneurship is quite a big thing within big companies, we’re drafting up a plan to give them a taste of the lean startup. Setting up innovation bootcamps, break out offices as we speak.

Idealabs: accelerator

Octopin, HSHMRK and Cotribute - 3 startups participated in our Acceleration program. A program that guides you from an idea to a business in 3 months. It’s now January, that means our program came to an end. One of the main goals of an accelerator is to launch an MVP in this short period of time. A minimum viable product, that can be tested on the market. Build, measure and learn, an infinite loop for a startup. We are very proud that all of our startups succeeded in this mission. Things that contributed to that mission:(second half of the program, first half contributions

  • Building like crazy men
  • We got visits by: Bart De Waele, Alexander Van De Rostyne, Fred Delafaille, Steve Schlafmann, Bruno Segers, Kris Hoet, Maarten Van Herck, Bart Becks
  • We held a Pitching Session at Coffeelabs. Startups could pitch in front of an unknown audience
  • We went for a whole week to London for Internet Week, you can check out the recap on slideshare
  • We had a Business Plan/Investment seminar powered by the nice folks of Deloitte
  • Wouter (Octopin) & Cedric (idealabs) went to LeWeb in Paris

Let’s take a look at the progress every startup made:

Octopin

It’s going pretty hard for Wouter. Octopin is a full marketing suite for brands on Pinterest. At this moment Octopin is tested and used by the likes of KLM, Heinz, Volkswagen, Van Marcke. Wouter has been very busy finetuning his product, getting everything ready to conquer the world of Pinterest. We tested out the product, and came up with this pinterest campaign. He got the chance to speak at the STIMAC2012 congress. As a 19 year old, he had to share his insights on being a young entrepreneur. Take a look at his talk. Wouter entered the Go West competition held by iMinds. He won a ticket to the US! Making it possible to conquer the American market. After all, 70 % of the pinterest users are located in the US. To keep growing, he received a follow up investment from idealabs. Make sure to follow him on Twitter / Facebook.

HSHMRK

Maarten hit some rough patches with HSHMRK. He broke down the initial idea. Trying to find the essence that could make HSHMRK a killer webapp. He did tons of customer development, and got himself surrounded by talented people (Tom De Ruyck - Insites, en Nathan - Datacruncher). Once convinced by the full potential, they continued building the product. At the moment they are finalizing everything and are getting ready to launch. Make sure to follow them on Twitter / Facebook.

Cotribute

Louis en Setareh had a bit of a bumpy ride with CoTribute. They changed their name a few times, but went all-in with CoTribute. They managed to launch their first MVP on the first of november. People could now create online memorial pages for their loved ones. They got Belgian press coverage and thought they were finally off. Reality came hard after tons of customer interviews. It's a though sector to get the word out. Considering every option, they decided to pivot. CoTribute is a platform for great stories by great people. Louis & Setareh are taking thing slower, make sure to check their website once in a while. Make sure to follow them on Twitter / Facebook.
